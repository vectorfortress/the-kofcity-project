How it works

A simple delivery flow designed for speed, reliability, and low-end smartphones.

1

Customer places an order

Parents, households, or schools choose meals or delivery services and select MTN MoMo, card, or cash on delivery.

2

Vendor prepares the order

The vendor receives the request, accepts it, prepares the food or goods, and marks it ready for pickup.

3

Rider delivers

A rider accepts the delivery, picks up the order, and updates the delivery status along the way.

4

Delivery is confirmed

The customer or school confirms receipt, and the platform tracks payment and payouts.

Simple payment options

KofCity supports the payment methods customers already use.

MTN MoMo

Primary digital payment option for launch in Koforidua.

Card Payments

Debit and credit card payments through a Ghana-supported payment gateway.

Cash on Delivery

Customers can pay when the rider delivers the order.

Pilot launch plan

The first launch will focus on Koforidua Central, then expand to nearby communities.

Launch Area

Koforidua Central, with planned expansion to Effiduase, Jumapo, Nsawam, and other Eastern Region towns.

Initial Partners

Start with 2–3 schools, 3–5 food vendors, and 3–5 riders to prove the model.

Technology Direction

Begin with a mobile-friendly web app, then expand into Android and iOS apps in Phase 2.
