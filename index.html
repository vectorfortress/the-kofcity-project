Simple order flow

The launch version uses clear status-based tracking with SMS or WhatsApp updates.

1
Order placed.
Customer, parent, or school places an order and chooses MoMo, card, or cash on delivery.
2
Vendor prepares.
The vendor receives the order, accepts it, prepares the food, and marks it ready.
3
Rider delivers.
A rider accepts the delivery, picks it up, and marks it out for delivery.
4
Receipt confirmed.
The customer or school confirms delivery. Cash orders are logged and payouts are tracked.

Payment options for Ghana

KofCity supports common local payment methods from the beginning.

MTN MoMo

Primary digital payment method for launch in Koforidua.

Cash on Delivery

Customers can pay the rider in person at drop-off.

Card Payments

Debit and credit card payments through Paystack or Flutterwave.

Vodafone Cash

Planned mobile money option for broader customer support.

AirtelTigo Money

Additional mobile money integration for future coverage.

Admin Payouts

Vendors and riders can be paid weekly or on request through MoMo or bank transfer.

Platform and technology plan

Start with a responsive web platform, then expand into Android and iOS mobile apps.

Phase 1 — Web Platform

  • Responsive website for desktop and mobile browsers
  • Registration forms for all user types
  • Admin dashboard for users, orders, and delivery zones
  • Status-based order tracking with SMS/WhatsApp notifications
  • MTN MoMo and cash on delivery support

Phase 2 — Mobile Apps

  • Android app first, then iOS app
  • Real-time map tracking for customers and delivery agents
  • In-app ordering and payment
  • Push notifications for order updates
  • Expansion to more Eastern Region communities

Pilot launch plan

The first launch should focus on proving the model with a manageable group of schools, vendors, riders, and customers.

1
Define delivery zones and pricing.
2
Approach food vendors in Koforidua.
3
Register 2–3 pilot schools.
4
Recruit 3–5 delivery agents.
5
Launch, test, improve, and expand.
